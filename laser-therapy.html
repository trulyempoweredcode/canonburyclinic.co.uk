Laser Therapy

Two specialist laser systems — one you won’t find at any other UK osteopathic practice.

High-Powered Laser

MiS MLS Class 4 Laser — Exclusive to Canonbury Clinic

Canonbury Clinic is the only osteopathic practice in the UK equipped with a high-powered MiS MLS Class 4 laser. This is not a standard therapy laser — it delivers very high instantaneous peak powers while maintaining carefully controlled average power, giving it clinical capabilities that lower-powered devices simply cannot match.

The MLS (Multiwave Locked System) technology synchronises two therapeutic wavelengths to produce a combined anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and regenerative effect. It penetrates deep into tissue, accelerating cellular repair and reducing pain from the very first session.

The result is a treatment that is both remarkably powerful and completely safe for the patient — a combination that makes it uniquely effective for stubborn, chronic conditions that have not responded to other approaches.

Low-Level Laser

Low-Level Laser Therapy (LLLT)

Also known as cold laser therapy, LLLT uses lower-intensity light to enhance cellular energy production at a mitochondrial level. This stimulates the body’s natural repair mechanisms, promotes tissue regeneration, and reduces inflammation — all without generating heat or causing discomfort.

LLLT is particularly effective for osteoarthritis, tendinitis, and a wide range of musculoskeletal injuries. It dovetails naturally with osteopathic treatment, especially for patients presenting with acute pain where hands-on techniques alone may not be enough.

At Canonbury Clinic, LLLT is included as part of your osteopathy session whenever it is clinically appropriate — no additional charge, no separate appointment.

What It Treats

Conditions That Respond to Laser Therapy

Both laser systems are effective across a broad range of musculoskeletal and inflammatory conditions:

  • Osteoarthritis (knees, hips, shoulders, hands)
  • Tendinitis and tendinopathy
  • Acute and chronic back pain
  • Neck pain and whiplash injuries
  • Frozen shoulder and rotator cuff injuries
  • Plantar fasciitis
  • Sports injuries and muscle strains
  • Post-surgical rehabilitation
