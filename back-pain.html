Understanding Your Pain

What Causes Back Pain?

Back pain is one of the most common problems we see, and most of us will experience it at some point in our lives. The important thing to know is that pain doesn’t necessarily mean something serious is wrong — but it does mean your body is telling you something needs attention.

Often, back pain doesn’t have one simple cause. It can result from a combination of factors including poor posture, repetitive strain, muscle imbalances, or wear and tear over time. Some of the specific conditions linked to back pain include:

  • Muscle spasm — sudden, involuntary tightening that can be extremely painful
  • Spondylosis — age-related wear and tear of the spinal joints
  • Sciatica — pain radiating down the leg from a compressed nerve
  • Slipped disc — when a spinal disc bulges and presses on surrounding nerves
  • General wear and tear — gradual degeneration that builds up over years

The sooner you get the correct treatment, the less time you spend suffering. That’s why accurate diagnosis is the first thing we focus on.
